Photo op

Overview

A mobile phone app that marks “selfie” locations on a map. Users will be able to identify new spots by uploading their photo and tagging the location. The idea is for users to be able to easily identify fun and interesting places to take photos nearby, for example street art murals, flowers in bloom, cool mirrors in a cafe etc. Users can also rate locations out of 5 stars, and tag identifying features (ie: mirror, art, nature) so locations can be filtered.

Motivation

Keeping up with social media trends is important to a large chunk of the population. Finding somewhere fun to take photos of yourself and with your friends to post is often a pastime on it’s own. It’s not unusual to see an influencer post a photo in front of a street art mural, for example, and see that location tag on instagram explode in popularity - not because of the specific influencer themselves, they just made the location known, but because people love taking cool photos. This app will be an easier way to immediately know what locations around you could be a good backdrop for your selfies.

Description

Users upload a photo of a good selfie backdrop and tag the location. They will also be asked to rate it out of 5 stars and tag it according to criteria like type (mirror, mural, nature, art), colours, indoor/outdoor, and accessibility.
When you open the app it has marked locations nearby. You can change the distance settings, as well as filter locations according to star rating and other criteria.

Tools and Technologies

This will most likely be a phone app, cross platform for iOS and Android. Ideally we hook into an existing and popular map application/framework and just build the platform for tagging and sharing locations. We will likely have everything we need for development and testing since it’s unusual for people to not have phones and the dev team will likely have a variety of cells for us to test on.

Skills Required

As far as hardware goes we should be okay, for software we will need knowledge on how to build mobile apps that connect to a server which stores profiles and the locations people tag in a fairly robust database.

Outcome

If the project is successful and the platform takes off, there will likely be a noticable increase in quality to your average selfie posted online. It will also encourage people to get out and find/visit beautiful parts of the places they live or are travelling to.
